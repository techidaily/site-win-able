 Unlike Google Sheets, Microsoft Excel doesn't enable autosave by default. You can fix that easily as long as you have a Microsoft 365 subscription. We're going to show you how to autosave Microsoft Excel workbooks to OneDrive.

 If you keep hitting Control+s (or Command+s on Mac) every few seconds, this method will ensure that you don't have any unsaved work in your Excel workbooks. It works by automatically saving your workbooks to OneDrive as you keep working on them.

Go ahead and open the OneDrive app, and follow the onscreen instructions to set it up. This just requires you to log in to OneDrive, and Microsoft will handle the rest.

##  Automatically Save Microsoft Excel Workbooks

 Now is the time to open Microsoft Excel and fire up a new workbook. Select "File."

![Click File](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/04/excel-auto-save-click-file.png) 

 Click "New" in the left pane.

![Click New](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/04/excel-auto-save-click-new.png) 

 Select "Blank workbook" or any other template from the list. This will open a new Excel workbook.

![Click Blank workbook](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/04/excel-auto-save-click-blank-workbook.png) 

 Right at the top, you'll see an option labeled "AutoSave." Click the toggle next to AutoSave to change it to "On."

![Click the toggle next to AutoSave](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/04/excel-auto-save-click-on.png) 

 You'll see a pop-up window that asks you where you want to save your workbook. Click "OneDrive."

![Click the toggle next to AutoSave](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/04/excel-auto-save-click-on.png) 

 Type the file name and click "OK."

![Click OK](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/04/excel-auto-save-click-ok.png) 

 That's all you need to do to enable autosave. From here on out, Excel will keep saving any modifications you make to this workbook. You'll just have to enable autosave once every time you start working on a new Excel workbook, and you won't need to save it again. You should note that you'll need an internet connection for autosave to work correctly and sync these workbooks across devices.

![A pop-up that confirms that your workbooks are being automatically saved](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/04/excel-auto-save-enabled.png)

 Open any of your workbooks in Microsoft Excel and click "File."
